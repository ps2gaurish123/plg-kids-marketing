# Narration Style

Use this style for PLG Kids story narrations and child-facing video voiceovers.

## Voice

- Voice: `en-IN-NeerjaExpressiveNeural`
- Accent: warm Indian-accent English
- Delivery: gentle, expressive, reassuring, parent-trust friendly
- Feel: children's story narration, patient teacher, soft bedtime-story warmth

## Pacing

- Slow the narration slightly for young children.
- Keep pauses clear between ideas.
- Avoid rushing lesson names, character names, and final calls to action.
- Prefer soft emphasis over dramatic intensity.

Suggested SSML direction:

```xml
<voice name="en-IN-NeerjaExpressiveNeural">
  <prosody rate="-12%" pitch="-1st" volume="soft">
    Narration text goes here.
  </prosody>
</voice>
```

## Tone Rules

- Speak like a kind storyteller, not an advertisement announcer.
- Use simple, clear sentences.
- Make the child feel safe, curious, and encouraged.
- Keep emotional moments warm, not scary.
- For mystery or problem-solving stories, create curiosity without harsh suspense.

## Final CTA

End narration with a clear CTA that includes:

```text
Start learning with PLG Kids at plg-kids.com
```

Alternative softer ending:

```text
Let your child speak, learn, and grow with confidence. Visit plg-kids.com.
```
